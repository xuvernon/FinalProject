Wondering how to grow honeysuckle?

Honeysuckle is known for its sweetly scented flowers. They are a heat tolerant plant that can grow almost anywhere. Their flowers can bloom in gorgeous bright pinks, oranges, yellows or whites, and some varieties have a unique two-colored flower.

    steps:

  • step 1 : planting

    • Plant your honeysuckle in early spring after any threat of frost has passed. Choose a sunny location where your soil is moist and excess water can drain off.

  • step 2 : condition

    • Ideally, full sun is best for them. Even though honeysuckle can tolerate a partially-shaded area, without sun, it may not bloom as much and could lose its leaves. Be sure you're planting your honeysuckle in organically rich and very well-drained soil. It should be moist but not soggy, as overly watered soil will become problematic.

  • step 3 : addtional care

    • Some honeysukle vines may require additional winter care. The more tender or tropical varieties will be most damaged by harsh winter weather. Carefully triming, planting close to supports and adding thick layers of mulch around the roots and at the base can help protect your plant.

    If you're planting your honeysuckle to climb, and you're not planting it against a house or other structure, you'll need support structures in place for the plant to grow up. Be sure to do this before you plant your honeysuckle. Once they are set up, plants should be about 6 - 12 inches out from the support.

Tips For Healthier Plants

  • Tip 1. Honeysuckle plants should be well (but not over) watered and mulched.
  • Tip 2. Add layers of compost each spring.
  • Tip 3. Pruning depends on the type of honeysuckle you plant. Be sure you know which variety you have. -- for example, common honeysuckle blooms on side shoots from the previous season, so if you prune it in the spring, you would eliminate your flowers.
  • Tip 4. If you plant honeysuckle in fertile soil, you may not need to fertilize.
