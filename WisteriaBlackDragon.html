Wondering how to grow a wistera?

Wisteria black dragon are heavily blooming from Spring to early/mid Summer. They will lose all their leaves in Autumn, then fresh new foliage appears again each Spring. 'Black Dragon' is a Wisteria with dark purple, lightly-fragrant flowers that flowers appear in succession from the vine base.

    Steps:

  • Step 1: Planting

    • Plant your wisteria in summer or spring. Plant them in a place withe full sun or light and well-drained soil.

  • Step 2: Condition

    • This plant thrives best in full sun. As for watering, soil should be kept evenly moist and watered regularly. Most plants like 1 inch of water a week during the growing season, but take care not to over water, as overly watered soil will become problematic.

  • Step 3: Additional Care

    • To enhance flowering this plant make sure to give it ample space for the roots to 'run' and prune it rigorously twice a year.

    Wisteria needs a lot of care and pruning every flowering season.

    Tips For A Healthier Plant

  1. Plant them in fertile, well-drained soil.
  2. Prepare soil in an area two to three feet in diameter and 18 to 24 inches deep.
  3. Gardeners usually have to prune their Wisteria to keep it from getting too large, but a good pruning can also encourage growth
  4. To help your Wisteria grow taller, give it something vertical to climb up.
