Wondering how to grow a Jade Plant?

Jade plant features thick, woody stems and glossy green, fleshy, oblong leaves up to two inches long. Happily, this low-maintenance plant lives a long time. They are well adapted for indoor growing and ideal for people desiring low-maintenance houseplants.

    Steps:

  • Step 1: Planting

    • Jade plantes tend to thrive in warm, dry climates and do not mind a little neglect. To start planting them you neet to fill the bottom one-third of the container with pre-moistened potting mix, then position your plant inside and backfill with more pre-moistened potting mix.

  • Step 2: Condition

    • They like direct sunlight. Most succulents prefer at least 6 hours of sun per day, so try to place them near a south or east facing window. Provide a very well-draining soil. They do not do well when sitting in constantly moist soil, so let the top 1 to 2 inches of soil dry out between waterings. This will probably mean watering once every 2 to 3 weeks. Use a container that has a drainage hole and avoid glass containers (such as mason jars or terrariums).

  • Step 3: Additional Care

    • Like many plants, the lowest leaves on the stem (closest to the potting mix) will eventually shrivel up and drop. This is normal and nothing to worry about. If the topmost leaves are dying, it could indicate overwatering, pests, or disease.

    Tips For A Healthier Plant

  1. The number one mistake many people make with succulents is overwatering them. So avoid overwatering your Jada Plant.
  2. If the soil stays consistently wet every day, the plant may eventually die.
  3. Fertilize your succulents at least once a year.
  4. Jade plants prefer loose, rocky soil and need nutrients to grow well.
